01 / About

Problem-solving first.
Everything else supports it.

I enjoy the part of software engineering where a vague or difficult problem becomes a clear system: inputs, constraints, decisions, tests and a result that actually works.

My university and personal projects have taken me from low-level digital logic and compiler work to C++ algorithms, MATLAB applications and a database-backed Ruby on Rails website. When I encounter an unfamiliar stack, I identify what the project needs, research the relevant concepts and learn by building.

I’m currently continuing that process during the university break by strengthening my HTML, CSS and JavaScript skills. Front-end design is not my main interest, but becoming capable across the full path from logic to interface makes me a more complete engineer.
